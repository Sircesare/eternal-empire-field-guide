The Beginner's Path

Follow this road to grow safely inside . Check off each milestone as you complete it.

For New Players
⚠️ Read This Before Anything Else

Do This First — Right Now

▾
1
Complete the Tutorial — Every Step
Don't skip anything. The tutorial builds your first Farm, Barracks, and Archery Range — all of which you need immediately. It also gives you free resources and speed-ups. Players who skip it fall behind from minute one.
2
Never Let Resource Buildings Sit Idle
Every Farm, Sawmill, and Stonemason should be producing at all times. Collect them the moment they're ready and queue the next production immediately. Resources are the engine of everything — buildings, troops, research. An idle building is a wasted minute.
3
Always Be Training Troops
Queue troops the moment you log in. The moment a batch finishes, queue another. A full Barracks running 24/7 is more valuable than any single upgrade. You can't fight, defend, or mine without troops — never let your queues go empty.
4
Always Be Researching Something
The Academy should never be idle. Start with City research — farming, lumber, construction speed. Every research node gives a permanent boost. Early on, prioritize City research first, then Nobles. Shift into Military research when your troop tiers, defenses, or war role require it.
5
Complete Daily Goals Every Single Day
Daily goals give Veteran Honors, resources, and XP. These are free rewards that take minutes to claim. Missing them is leaving power on the table every single day. Log in, complete goals, log out — at minimum.
6
Stay Below Keep 10 Until You Are Ready
When you upgrade to Keep 10 a two-day countdown begins — after 48 hours your beginner shield is permanently removed and you are open to attack. Before you hit K10, make sure your economy is producing reliably, your troops are trained, and you are inside . Use those two days to prepare. Once the shield drops it never comes back.
The habit that separates strong players from weak ones: Every time you open the game — collect resources, queue troops, queue research, complete daily goals. Do it in that order, every session, without fail.

Dynasty Strategy

🧬 Breeding Pair Strategy

▾
Same-Line Breeding — Pure Bloodline
EN
Enforcer Line
Captain + Marshal → produce Justicar
▾

Pair Captain × Marshal to breed the strongest Justicar bloodline. Both parents use Swordsmen + Archers so all inherited combat traits are relevant to the child's role. Best for rally-focused families.

GU
Guardian Line
Knight + Paladin → produce Sentinel
▾

Pair Knight × Paladin for pure Guardian bloodline. Both run Cavalry + Crossbows so trait inheritance stays combat-relevant. Ideal for defense-focused players who anchor rallies.

HA
Harbinger Line
Subjugator + Conqueror → produce Vanquisher
▾

Pair Subjugator × Conqueror for a strong Vanquisher-focused breeding path. Axemen + Catapult traits stack hard against Cavalry-heavy enemies. Eternal Empire's anti-cavalry specialists come from this line.

⚠️ Do Not Marry Only for Role Labels. A weaker same-line pairing is not automatically better than a stronger cross-line pairing with superior Mythical traits, rarity, and long-term breeding value. Post both nobles in Discord before permanent marriage decisions.
Cross-Line Breeding — Trait Stacking
💡 Cross-Breeding Strategy: Pairing nobles from different combat lines lets you stack traits from both pools into one child. Example: Marshal × Paladin child can inherit both Enforcer and Guardian combat traits — giving a noble a wider trait set to pass down for generations. The tradeoff is you need an open role slot for the desired output role.
Marshal × Paladin
Enforcer + Guardian traits. Great for producing versatile high-trait nobles.
Paladin × Conqueror
Guardian + Harbinger traits. Produces nobles with anti-cavalry and defensive traits stacked.
Marshal × Conqueror
Enforcer + Harbinger traits. Max offensive trait pool for aggressive rally builds.
Any Combat × Explorer
Passes dungeon payout traits into combat lines — useful niche for trait diversity.
Non-Combat Cross Breeding — Economy Bloodlines
💡 Economy Breeding: Non-combat nobles benefit from cross-breeding too. Mixing Barter, Delve, and Automation traits produces well-rounded economy nobles with stronger trait pools than pure same-line breeding.
Merchant × Explorer
Passes Barter + Delve traits. Produces versatile economy nobles with both trading and dungeon bonuses.
Agent × Collier
Passes Barter + Automation traits. Useful for producing nobles with both trade and mining trait depth.
Any Combat × Merchant
Passes trade traits into a combat noble's bloodline. Niche use — only if you need charm or trade bonuses on a combat line.
Explorer × Collier
Passes Delve + Automation traits. Best for producing Ranger or Prospector with a wider inherited trait pool.
Risk Assessment

⚠️ Inbreeding — Risks vs Rewards

▾
🔴 Eternal Empire Standard: Inbreeding is a last resort technique. Never do it without understanding the consequences. Always clear with leadership if you're considering it for a key bloodline noble.
Why Players Do It
When a noble has rare Mythical traits that can't be sourced from outside families, inbreeding locks those traits inside your bloodline. Generations of inbreeding can stack those same traits deeper and deeper — producing nobles with a highly concentrated trait pool that outside breeding would dilute.
The Cons
💀 Child always gets the permanent Inbred trait — cannot be rerolled, stays forever
💀 If parents are siblings, child can be born as low as Runt quality
💀 Inbred trait can itself be inherited by future generations — compounding the problem
💀 Weakens the bloodline long-term if overused — diminishing quality returns
The Pros
✅ Keeps rare Mythical traits locked inside your family
✅ Can stack high trait counts across generations without outside dilution
✅ Useful when no outside family has comparable quality nobles to marry in

🗺️ Battlefield Slot Positioning

Before battle starts you can reposition battalions in the green deployment zone. Position matters — wrong placement gets your ranged killed instantly.

🛡️
Front Line — Tanks
Swordsmen, Cavalry, or Axemen go front. They absorb damage and engage enemy tanks directly. Position them toward the enemy gates.
🏹
Mid Line — DPS
Archers and Crossbowmen go behind tanks. They deal damage from range while tanks hold the enemy in place. Keep them protected — losing DPS early loses the fight.
💣
Support — Catapults
Catapults position behind DPS. They lob boulders over the frontline targeting Cavalry. Keep them far back — they are slow and fragile up close.
🏯
Last — Trebuchets
Trebuchets deploy ONLY after all enemy armies are cleared. Position them toward walls. Never advance Trebs into active combat — they will be destroyed immediately.
💡 Pro Tip: Tap the torch in the middle of your army to move all battalions at once to a new position. Use individual battalion taps to fine-tune placement before advancing.

Combat Roles

Nobles in PvP

Your noble is the engine of your army. The right noble + troop pairing doesn't just improve performance — it can punch your army into the tier above what your raw troop level suggests. The wrong pairing does the opposite. Know the three lines cold.

▾
ENFORCER Captain → Marshal → Justicar
▼
Troops: ⚔️ Swordsmen 🏹 Archers

PvP Role: The Enforcer line is your frontline army-clearer. Captains open fights by engaging enemy armies directly — their talent trees buff Swordsmen damage and Archer range simultaneously. Marshals step up to rally leadership, amplifying the whole army's output for coordinated strikes. Justicars are the Enforcer pinnacle, best for large multi-army rallies where their passive bonuses stack with allied nobles.

Punching Up a Tier: Deep Combat talent investment on an Enforcer can make T2 Swordsmen perform like underpowered T3 in PvP — especially against cavalry-heavy defenders. Swordsmen hard-counter Cavalry; Enforcer talents amplify that matchup. Always lead your army-clear phase with an Enforcer.

Equipment Slots: Helm · Chest · Ring · Weapon · Off-Hand · Accessory. Focus Weapon and Chest pieces on combat nobles — these give the strongest army damage and health bonuses respectively.
GUARDIAN Knight → Paladin → Sentinel
▼
Troops: 🐴 Cavalry 🎯 Crossbowmen

PvP Role: The Guardian line specializes in mobile strike and defensive anchor. Cavalry ignores watchtowers entirely — a major PvP advantage since they bypass tower garrisons and crash directly into enemy armies. Crossbowmen deal AoE bolt damage that shreds densely packed battalions. Knights are fast flankers; Paladins and Sentinels become walls that hold gates while the rest of your army pushes through.

Punching Up a Tier: Cavalry's tower-ignore mechanic means a well-invested Guardian can defend longer than higher-tier infantry armies that get bogged down by garrison fire. On offense, Knight-led Cavalry armies reach the back of an enemy city faster than any other noble line.

Equipment Slots: Prioritize Off-Hand (shield variants) for defensive Paladin/Sentinel builds. Weapon slot for offensive Knight builds. Sentinels especially benefit from Chest pieces with high defense bonuses.
HARBINGER Subjugator → Conqueror → Vanquisher
▼
Troops: 🪓 Axemen 💣 Catapults

PvP Role: Harbingers are Eternal Empire's anti-cavalry specialists and city assault force. Axemen hard-counter Cavalry — the most common heavy unit in PvP — dealing critical hit damage against Heavy Tank units. Catapults have unique mechanics: they can target gates directly AND fire over walls, letting them suppress tower and platform garrisons from range. Conquerors unlock the Torture mechanic for captured nobles.

Punching Up a Tier: Axemen's hard counter to Cavalry means a Harbinger army can beat a Guardian army running higher-tier troops in a straight matchup. If your enemy is cavalry-heavy, deploy Harbinger first — the critical hit bonus against Heavy Tanks dramatically shifts the outcome even at a troop tier disadvantage.

Equipment Slots: Conqueror and Vanquisher benefit most from Weapon slots with attack bonuses versus Heavy Tanks — these amplify Axemen's built-in Cavalry counter. Pair with armor resistant to whatever element your enemy brings.
⚠️ Common Misconception: Trebuchets and Rockbreakers are NOT boosted by Harbinger nobles (Subjugator/Conqueror/Vanquisher). Harbingers only boost Axemen and Catapults. Never assign Trebuchets expecting noble bonuses to apply — they won't.
💡 Eternal Empire Doctrine: Scout before you send. Enforcer clears armies → Harbinger handles cavalry and gates → Guardian flanks and holds. Use all three lines in coordination. A solo army at a fortified city is almost always a loss.
Officers & Engineers

Champions in PvP

Champions are elite officers assigned to your noble's army as sub-commanders. They do two critical things in PvP: they expand your army's total battalion count and they bring Epic abilities that can swing a fight mid-combat. Never field a PvP army without champions assigned.

▾
🗡️ Officer Class

Leads troop battalions — Infantry, Ranged, or Mounted. Best class for PvP since troop armies dominate player combat. Each Officer champion adds one troop battalion slot to your army. Passive bonuses typically buff the troop type they specialize in.

⚙️ Engineer Class

Leads machine battalions — Catapults, Trebuchets, Ballista. Each Engineer champion adds one machine battalion slot. Pair with a Harbinger noble for maximum siege output on city assaults.

📦 Extra Battalion Slots

Each champion you assign adds one battalion slot beyond what your noble holds alone. More slots means more troops in the fight. In PvP, army size directly impacts how many casualties both sides take before one breaks — maxing your champion roster is a force multiplier that costs nothing once you have the champions. Research and building upgrades also increase troop count per battalion; don't neglect those either.

⚡ Epic Champion Abilities

Higher-level champions unlock Epic abilities that activate in combat — not passive stat bonuses, but active effects that fire during battle. The most impactful in PvP:

🌀 AoE Slow
Slows all enemy battalions in an area simultaneously. In PvP this is devastating — it delays enemy armies from reaching your lines, giving your ranged units extra volleys before melee contact. Against Cavalry-heavy armies that rely on speed to crash through your front line, an AoE slow can turn an otherwise losing fight into a win.

Epic abilities unlock through leveling and promotion. Prioritize leveling champions assigned to your main PvP army first. Hero XP is earned through NPC camp clears and daily goals.

⚠️ Match Champion Class to Army Type: Don't assign an Engineer champion to a troop-only army — their battalion capacity bonus doesn't apply to infantry or ranged units. Check each champion's class before assigning and confirm their specialty matches your noble's troop type.
Battalion Equipment

Elements & Equipment

Battalion Equipment is crafted at the Blacksmith and assigned to individual troop battalions — not to your noble. The weapon slot is the most important decision: it sets your battalion's element. Elements interact in a triangle that deals bonus damage to the counter element and reduced damage against the resistant element.

▾
🗡️ Weapon Slot Sets the Element

Equip a Fire Sword on Swordsmen and they become Fire Swordsmen. Give Archers Frost Arrows and they become Frost Archers. The troop type doesn't change — only the element. Armor pieces do NOT change element: they only reduce incoming damage from the matching element, which is a separate defensive layer.

🗺️ NPC Faction Element Guide
🔥
Cinderbone Orcs — Fire Element
Cinderbone units wield Fire damage and carry Fire-resistant armor. Counter them with Frost weapons — Frost deals bonus damage against Fire. Equip Frost armor on your battalions to resist their incoming Fire damage.
❄️
Graveborn — Frost Element
Graveborn units wield Frost damage and carry Frost-resistant armor. Lightning weapons are the confirmed counter to Frost. Equip Frost-resistant armor to reduce incoming Frost damage.
⚙️
Human NPCs — Variable
Human NPC armies can carry Fire or Frost battalion equipment in certain events and quests. Check the Advantage button when inspecting the target — green arrows mean you have the elemental edge, red arrows mean you don't.
⚠️ Correction: Sparksteel-forged weapons deal Physical element damage, but in-game data confirms Physical has no elemental advantage against anything — it does not counter Frost. If you're facing a Frost-heavy enemy, the confirmed counter is Lightning, not Sparksteel/Physical.
Offensive Play

Attack Strategy

A successful PvP assault on a player city follows a sequence. Deviating from the sequence wastes your army. Every step below exists for a reason — don't skip any of them.

▾
🚨 Eternal Empire War Rule: Do not launch city attacks, open gates, capture Nobles, or start retaliation without leadership approval during active diplomacy, war, or shard events. When in doubt — ask in Discord first.
1
Target Selection — Attack Offline Players

The biggest force multiplier in PvP isn't troop tier — it's timing. Attacking when a player is offline means no real-time reinforcement calls, no manual army repositioning, and no emergency peace shield. Check activity before committing an army. For coordinated alliance strikes, use Intel from a Bribed noble to identify targets who haven't been active — their armies will be unmanaged and their alliance slower to respond.

2
Noble Intel + Gate Opening

Before sending any army, send a Noble ahead to the enemy keep to Greet their Nobles. Use Silver to Bribe one of their Nobles — success applies the "Bribed" trait and unlocks two follow-up actions. Bribe cost scales relative to how much Silver the target noble carries — a broke noble is cheap to bribe.

📋 Get Intel
Full enemy report: all troop counts, every family member location, and every alliance member city location. Share in alliance chat before coordinated strikes. Report is a snapshot — acts on it quickly before it goes stale.
🚪 Open Gates
Opens the city gates — skipping the wall breach phase entirely. Only visible to you. The gates stay open until the next attack lands, then close after that battle ends. Whoever attacks first gets the full advantage. Both Bribe and Open Gates can be leveled up in the Noble's Intelligence skill tree — invest here on your designated spy noble.

Alternative: Blackmail works at no Silver cost if you've previously Boinked their noble and produced a bastard — this gives them the "Ashamed" trait which also unlocks Get Intel and Open Gates.

3
Clear Enemy Armies First

Your first wave is always an army-clearing wave. Deploy battalions toward enemy field armies in the green zone — not toward the walls. Enemy armies left alive will peel your siege off walls and destroy them. Use Enforcer nobles (Swordsmen + Archers) and Guardian nobles (Cavalry + Crossbowmen) for this phase. Counter whatever the defender is running based on the troop triangle — scout first if you can.

4
Siege Phase — Catapults vs Trebuchets

Once enemy armies are cleared, advance your siege. These two siege units are not interchangeable — each has a defined job:

💣 Catapults
Target gates directly. Fire over walls — clears tower and platform garrisons from range without entering their fire arc. Direct toward gates. Harbinger nobles boost Catapult effectiveness.
🏯 Trebuchets
Walls only. Never advance into melee — destroyed instantly in close combat. Deploy after armies are cleared. Keep behind your melee line. Direct toward wall segments, not gates.
5
Guild Capture Near Capitals

Capturing guild structures near a county capital provides bonus combat damage in that region. This is a pre-attack bonus requiring coordination — not a mid-battle mechanic. Before a major PvP campaign in a county, coordinate with Eternal Empire leadership to capture and hold nearby guild structures before the attack launches.

6
Breach the Keep

The objective is the keep — not resources, not noble captures. Everything above feeds into one goal: getting your infantry through a gate or breach and into the keep. Position your fastest melee battalion toward the gate early so they advance the moment a gate falls or walls are breached.

⚠️ Eternal Empire Rule: Never send a solo army at a fortified city. Coordinate with leadership. Solo attacks on active players with walls up are almost always losses regardless of troop tier advantage.
City Defense

Defense Strategy

Defense is about overlapping fields of fire, denying enemy routes, and making your keep as expensive to reach as possible. Each structure type has hard rules on what it can and cannot garrison — ignore them and your whole setup is wasted.

▾
🏗️ Structure Rules — Know These Cold
🗼 Garrison Towers (Watchtowers)

Place towers every other wall segment — never back-to-back. Back-to-back towers let enemies climb one and immediately reach the next through the connected wall. Tower doors must face inward — doors facing outward let enemies use them to scale your walls. Towers can be converted to Blocking Towers (no door, no garrison) to permanently close wall gaps.

✅ Crossbowmen ✅ Archers ❌ Catapults (can't enter towers) ❌ Infantry (no range value)
⛺ Platforms

Place most platforms inside your walls — this way they can't be targeted until attackers breach a gate. Non-ranged troops garrisoned on platforms will run off to engage enemies when they get in range — don't waste platform slots on infantry unless they're outside the walls as a first-contact force. Upgrading platforms increases troop capacity and protection from enemy fire.

✅ Catapults (fire over walls) ✅ Archers (fire over walls) ❌ Crossbowmen (CANNOT shoot over walls)
📋 Defensive Doctrine
▶

Crossbows in towers, Catapults on platforms. This is the golden rule. Crossbows deal AoE bolt damage and can fire from towers at enemies on walls or outside. Catapults on inside platforms fire over walls and hit gates — the most effective defensive siege placement.

▶

Never put Crossbowmen on platforms. They physically cannot shoot over walls from a platform position. Their AoE bolt damage is wasted if they can't fire at incoming enemies. This is one of the most common defensive mistakes.

▶

Archers can fire over walls from both towers and platforms. They're flexible — use them to fill whichever structure needs ranged coverage. Their Rain of Arrows ability deals splash damage to clustered enemy battalions.

▶

Morale boosts defense. The Keep has a boostable room that improves the damage of all garrisoned troops. Assign a noble to that room before any expected attack and ask alliance members to boost it — this is a free damage multiplier for your entire garrison.

▶

Reinforce immediately. If you're online during an attack, call for reinforcements in alliance chat. Reinforcing armies queue up — when one falls the next deploys automatically. A queue of 3–4 reinforcing armies can turn a losing defense into a victory.

⚠️ Tower Door Warning: Every garrison tower has a door. Enemy troops who reach that door can scale the wall and reach adjacent towers — bypassing your entire wall structure. Always point doors inward, or convert towers to Blocking Towers where you don't need a garrison.
Eternal Empire Intelligence

Tips & Techniques

Advanced strategies used by experienced Eternal Empire members. These are not in any tutorial — learn them here.

💰 The Overnight Silver + Food Loop ▼
🔴 Eternal Empire Technique: This loop runs while you sleep — wake up with food and silver already waiting. The moment you log back in collect your Barter Merits and immediately head to the Wandering Merchant for protected resources. Execute this every day and night for maximum effect.
Step by Step
Step 1 — Send Merchant to Level 40 Trading Post
Buy a large quantity of Food at a Level 40 Trading Post. This gives you a massive food stockpile AND approximately 200 Barter Merits from the trade — running overnight while you sleep.
Step 2 — Send Agent/Second Merchant to Level 30 Trading Post
Simultaneously send your second trade noble to a Level 30 Trading Post to sell resources. This runs in parallel and generates an additional 30,000–50,000 Silver overnight.
Step 3 — Wake Up & Take Merits to Wandering Merchant
You wake up with ~200 Barter Merits + 30–50k Silver + large food stockpile. Immediately take those Merits to the Wandering Merchant and trade for Protected Food or Protected Resources. This makes you virtually raid-proof — raiders can't loot protected resources.
⚠ Wandering Merchant — Key Facts:
  • 🗺️ Moves to a new random location every 4 hours — 4 active locations at any time on the map
  • ⚠️ If it moves mid-trade your trade may be cancelled — always check timing before sending your noble
  • 💎 Protected resource trades cost Barter Merits but give a 2x+ exchange rate on unprotected → protected resources
  • 🛡️ Protected trades take 1.5 to 3 hours — you can realistically complete 2 or more protected trades per day, not just overnight
  • 👑 Merchant needs Barter IV talent (T4, L40) for best WM trades. Agent needs Improved Barter III (T3, L30)
What You Get When Implemented Correctly
✅ Large food stockpile
✅ 30–50k Silver
✅ ~200 Barter Merits
✅ Protected resources = raid proof

The Level 40 Rule
Only promote past L40 if the noble has ALL of the following:
✅ 5 or more inherited traits total
✅ Minimum 2 Mythical traits — inherited, innate, or a combination of both
✅ Noble is a confirmed keeper — not a placeholder to be replaced
❌ Fewer than 5 inherited traits? Stop at 40 — this noble will be replaced.
❌ Less than 2 Mythical traits? Stop at 40 — Rubies and Emeralds are too valuable to waste.
❌ Lower quality noble? Stop at 40 — breed a better one and develop that instead.
Why Forts Are the Best XP Source
🏰 Forts Give the Highest XP Per Battle
Camps, armies and dungeons all give XP — but forts give significantly more per engagement than any other PvE target. The harder the fort, the bigger the XP reward. This makes fort farming the single fastest free leveling method in the game.
⚔️ Nobles Earn More XP In Their Role
Combat nobles earn bonus XP from combat actions. Always send your combat noble to attack the fort — not an economy noble filling in. Keep your noble in their lane and they level faster.
🪖 Use Trebuchets to Clear Forts Faster
Trebuchets break fort walls faster than any other unit, letting you defeat the fort more efficiently and move on to the next run sooner. Faster clears = more runs per session = more XP per hour. Never attack a fort wall without Trebuchets if you have them available.
📈 Progress to Harder Forts as Soon as Possible
XP rewards scale with fort difficulty. The moment you can reliably beat a harder fort tier, move up — staying on easy forts you one-shot is wasting XP potential every single run. If you can clear it in under a minute, you're ready for the next tier.
🔁 The Fort XP Loop
1 Attack the hardest fort you can reliably clear
2 Use Trebuchets to break walls faster
3 Collect XP — heal troops — repeat immediately
4 Unlock talent tiers with Merits to raise level cap
5 Move up to the next fort tier as soon as you can clear it
💡 Eternal Empire Rule: Fort farming is a daily habit, not a one-time grind. Run it every session. A noble leveled to L80 through consistent fort farming has dramatically stronger army buffs than a L40 noble with the same traits — the stat gains per level are significant.
🛡️ How to Never Be a Raid Target ▼
Raiders attack cities that show large amounts of unprotected resources. The overnight loop above solves this automatically — but here's the core principle:
✅ Protected Food from Wandering Merchant trades = cannot be looted
✅ Protected Resources from Wandering Merchant = cannot be looted
✅ Running trades overnight = resources are in transit, not sitting in your city
✅ Spending resources on buildings/research before logging = nothing to steal
💀 Sitting on large unprotected resource stockpiles while offline = prime raid target
💀 Leaving food unprotected = cavalry armies will drain your farms on their way in
🔴 Eternal Empire Rule: Before you log off — spend, protect, or trade everything. Leave nothing on the table for raiders.

Eternal Empire Code

Alliance Warfare Rules

These are not suggestions. These are the doctrines that make Eternal Empire the dominant force on the shard.

▾
01

Focus Fire — No Splitting

All members concentrate attacks on a single designated target at a time. Splitting damage lets enemies heal and recover. Coordinate in chat before every attack.

02

Reinforce Immediately

When an alliance member is under attack, reinforce without waiting. Your army in their castle is the difference between survival and destruction. Monitor chat constantly.

04

Rally Timing Is Sacred

Rallies must be coordinated with leadership. Sending a rally at the wrong time wastes momentum. Wait for the signal from leadership or designated rally leaders.

Report attacks, threats, and target status in alliance chat immediately. Silent members create gaps in defense. Eternal Empire wins through coordination.

06

No Resource Raiding

Eternal Empire does NOT support raiding player castles for resources. All aggression must be sanctioned by leadership and aimed at military targets only.

Prisoner Protocol

Noble Capture Rules

Win a battle and you may capture an enemy noble. How you handle prisoners reflects on all of Eternal Empire.

▾
⚔️ Capture Options
🔒 Hold
Keep the noble in your dungeon. Useful as leverage during negotiations.
💰 Ransom
Demand resources from the enemy family in exchange for release.
⚡ Torture
Locks the noble in their keep for 7 days — can't march, trade, or explore. Conqueror role only — requires Crime & Punishment talent.
🕊️ Release
Free the noble with no conditions. Can be used as a diplomatic gesture.
⚠ Jailbreak Warning: Enemy alliances can attempt to jailbreak your prisoners. Don't hold high-value nobles too long without a plan. Also note — Plague spell prevents noble movement including jailbreak attempts.
